Chapter Summaries

  • Act 1 Scene 1
  • The play opens with two men on stage, Gloucester and Kent
  • The men are discussing the aging King Lear and the division of his fortune and Kingdom once he steps down
  • Soon after the conversation begins it changes to a different topic, Gloucester introduces his son Edmund, who was standing nearby, and explains that Edmund is actually a bastard but non the less he loves him dearly
  • King Lear enters the stage in which is now set up as his throne room
  • He states that he is ready to divide his kingdom between his three daughters, and that they must all state how much they love him and the most loving will receive the best share
  • Two of his daughters, Regan and Goneril, play along and offer endearing speeches of there love which pleases Lear
  • When it comes to the youngest and favorite daughter Cordelia there is quite a different answer
  • Cordelia states that she can only love him as much as any daughter should love a father and that she will not make outlandish statements about her love in order to win his kingdom
  • Lear is furious by this and disowns Cordelia, opting to instead split the kingdom among the other two
  • Nearby the earl of Kent hears the conversations and objects to Lear about what he has done, however Lear is furious with him as well and bans him from the kingdom
  • Meanwhile the King of France and the duke of Burgundy plan on courting Cordelia, however once Lear informs them that she is no longer an heiress the duke wants nothing more to do with her, the King of France on the other hand is all the more enthralled with her
  • The two sisters scheme behind the scenes about the kingdom and how to wrestle all the power from there father
  • Act 1 Scene 2
  • Edmund reenters the stage and offers up a soliloquy
  • He explains his distress with society's treatment of bastards and adds his issues with his half-brother Edgar who is in line to receive all of their father's fortune as he is legitimate
  • Edmund devises a plan to create dissent between his father and half-brother
  • He forges a letter as Edgar stating Edgar's desire to murder Gloucester, their father
  • Gloucester spots the letter, which is part of Edmund's plan, and demands to see it
  • Once reading it Gloucester believes that Edgar is plotting his death
  • Edmund also goes to Edgar and lies saying that Gloucester is very angry with him and that Edgar should avoid him and be armed at all times
  • Edmund hopes this will further perpetuate Gloucester's belief that Edgar
  • Act 1 Scene 3
  • Lear and many of his knights have traveled to Goneril's home to visit and stay with her
  • The knights behavior as well as Lear's begins to upset Goneril to the point that she asks all her servants to treat them rudely in hopes to start a confrontation
  • Act 1 Scene 4
  • At Goneril's castle Kent has disguised himself in hopes of getting in with Lear
  • Kent pretends to be a peasant by the name of Caius and manages to convince Lear to let him work for him
  • Goneril's servants have become increasingly stubborn towards any requests of Lear's men
  • There is an encounter between one servant, Oswald, and Lear causing Lear to strike Oswald
  • Kent, in order to prove his allegiance to Lear, joins in and trips Oswald
  • Meanwhile the local Fool arrives and tells Lear that his decision with his power was ultimately wrong and that Goneril and Regan cannot control his kingdom
  • Following this Goneril finally approaches Lear about his knights behavior and tells him that some of them must go
  • Lear is furious and curses Goneril saying he regrets giving her any power
  • He then storms out with all of his knights to go stay with Regan
  • When they are all gone Goneril and her husband Albany argue, as Albany believes Goneril acted wrongly
  • Act 1 Scene 5
  • Lear sends the disguised Kent to Gloucester to give him a message
  • Meanwhile the Fool still harasses Lear about his decisions and states that Regan will treat him no better than Goneril
  • Frustrated, Lear calls out to the gods and asks them to keep him sane
  • Act 2 Scene 1
  • At Gloucester's home Edmund is informed that the duke of Cornwall and his wife Regan will soon be arriving to stay with Gloucester
  • The source, Curan, also tells Edmund that there have been some differences between the duke of Cornwall and the duke of Albany (Goneril's husband)
  • Edmund is pleased with what he learns as he plots to include the duke in his plans to oust Edgar
  • Edmund seeks out Edgar who is hiding and exclaims that the duke of Cornwall is coming and he is upset with Edgar
  • Edmund adds that Gloucester has found out where Edgar has been hiding and that Edgar should leave immediately
  • Edgar is confused, he then draws his sword and playfully tries to fight with Edmund
  • Edmund runs away and cuts his arm with his own sword, then he goes to Gloucester and tells him that Edgar stabbed him when he refused to help him kill Gloucester
  • Gloucester is furious, he sends his men to find Edgar
  • Cornwall and Regan finally arrive and Edmund takes the opportunity to tell them the lies about Edgar, which the two believe wholeheartedly
  • They also speculate that Edgar plots to murder Gloucester to take his wealth
  • Regan then discusses with Gloucester about how she should respond to the letters she received from Lear and Goneril
  • Act 2 Scene 2
  • The play shifts to outside of Gloucester's castle where Kent confronts Oswald again
  • Oswald doesn't remember Kent from the previous confrontation despite Kent still being disguised
  • Kent is very abrasive and rude and Oswald is in shock as he doesn't know why he is being treated that way
  • Kent then draws his sword and begins to attack Oswald
  • Oswald cries out and he is answered by help from Regan, Gloucester, and Cornwall
  • The three decide to put Kent in the stocks for the night for punishment
  • Gloucester is objective about this being that Kent is a servant of Lear, however he ultimately obliges
  • Once his is left by himself in the stocks he pulls out a letter from Cordelia, she claims that she will try to find a way to improve conditions in Britain
  • Act 2 Scene 3
  • While Kent sleeps in the stocks Edgar enters the stage
  • After avoiding capture for the time being he decides to increase his odds by disguising himself as a beggar
  • He vows to roam the countryside seeking food and shelter
  • Act 2 Scene 4
  • Lear finally arrives at Gloucester's castle with the Fool and one of his Knights
  • He sees Kent in the stocks and is appalled by how he is being treated
  • Lear learns from Kent that he was placed there by Regan and Cornwall
  • Lear then demands to speak with both of them about there treatment of Kent, however they decline and state that they are sick from traveling
  • Lear is quite insistent and finally gets his chance to speak to them
  • During their conversation Lear tells of Goneril's frustration with him when he was staying with her
  • However to Lear's surprise Regan supports Goneril as opposed to Lear, she also adds that perhaps Lear should return and apologize
  • Lear is upset and asks Regan to take him in, however she refuses
  • Soon Goneril arrives to the castle as well and the two sisters gang up on there father and demand that he lose half of his Knights at least before they will take him in
  • When he refuses their demands become even stricter causing him to become enraged and leave the castle
  • Lear heads outside into a brewing storm
  • Act 3 Scene 1
  • Kent has escaped the stocks and is now in desperate search for Lear
  • He runs into a knight and learns that Lear is nearby with the Fool
  • Kent tells the knight some secret information that he has learned about the dispute between Albany and Cornwall and the unrest in England
  • He sends the knight to Dover, a city near France, where he can find people that will help Lear. He also provides the knight with a ring that he shall present to Cordelia so she will know who has sent him
  • Act 3 Scene 2
  • Lear is wandering around in the storm almost deliriously with the Fool
  • He is still upset with his daughters and curses the gods and storms
  • The Fool suggests they return for shelter but Lear refuses
  • Eventually Kent catches up to them and urges Lear to seek shelter in a nearby hovel to which he finally obliges
  • Act 3 Scene 3
  • Gloucester and Edmund are on stage speaking
  • Gloucester tells Edmund about Cornwall, Goneril, and Regan's unfair treatment of Lear
  • He adds that when he suggested they search for Lear and offer him shelter the three were very angry, shut him up, and even took control of his castle
  • Gloucester then tells about having learned of a feud between Cornwall and Albany and an impending war between France and Britain
  • He feels that he must go seek out Lear in hopes of preventing any such thing occurring
  • Gloucester asks Edmund to keep his plans very secret so as to not get him harmed or even murdered for treachery
  • Edmund is thrilled by what has just happened, he can now tell the others about his father's treachery and when he is put to death inherit his father's fortune
  • Act 3 Scene 4
  • The play shifts back to Kent, Lear, and the Fool
  • They have arrived at the hovel, however Lear refuses to enter saying that in his current state he is oblivious to the storm
  • He then kneels down in prayer in which he resents his acts as King and his treatment of peasants and the poor
  • All of the sudden the Fool runs out of the hovel claiming that there is a spirit inside
  • Sure enough someone emerges, it is Edgar, disguised as Tom O'Bedlam
  • He claims he is being chased by the devil and that he has been possessed by fiends
  • Lear is not surprised by this, he then questions Tom as to what he was before becoming a beggar
  • Tom, or Edgar, explains that he was a very wealthy courtier, and that he spent all of his days having sex with beautiful women and drinking
  • Lear sympathizes with Edgar
  • Suddenly Gloucester shows up and begs Lear to return to the castle
  • Lear is reluctant, however he finally agrees with the urging of Kent
  • He decides to bring the disguised Edgar with him
  • Act 3 Scene 5
  • Back at the castle Cornwall plots revenge against Gloucester after learning of his treachery
  • Edmund acts surprised by his father's treachery, however he is really pleased
  • Cornwall gives Edmund the title of earl of Gloucester
  • He then sends him on a mission to find Gloucester and see if he is helping Lear so that he can then be subjected to punishment
  • Act 3 Scene 6
  • Gloucester brings the group to a shed on his property where he can keep them hidden while he finds them provisions
  • While Gloucester is gone the King seems to hold a mock trial of his daughters and uses the others as actors, he is very delusional
  • Gloucester suddenly returns after overhearing a plot to kill Lear, he tells Kent and then tells ho, that he must take Lear to Dover where he can be saved
  • All of the men leave the stage, however Edgar remains behind, he speaks about how his suffering is minuscule compared to Lear's
  • Act 3 Scene 7
  • Back inside the castle Cornwall and Goneril discuss the treason, they decide that she will go to her husband Albany to tell him of what they have learned
  • She decides that she will bring Edmund with her when she goes so that he wont be present when Gloucester is punished
  • Gloucester is then caught after helping Lear escape, he is brought in and then abused by Cornwall and Regan
  • Cornwall insists they hold a fair trial for him first
  • Gloucester confesses to his help of Lear, to which Cornwall punishes him by gouging out his eye and stomping on it
  • A servant steps in and demands it stop, however Cornwall is furious and duals the servant
  • Cornwall is wounded but Regan kills the servant before any harm can be done
  • Enraged, Cornwall gouges out the other eye
  • Gloucester calls out for help from Edmund, however he is informed that Edmund betrayed him and that he made a grave mistake by distrusting his loyal son Edgar
  • Cornwall and Regan then order Gloucester out of his house, blind, and send him to Dover
  • Regan and Cornwall leave the servants to kick out Gloucester, however they are appalled by what has occurred and help Gloucester
  • They then give him to Edgar, still disguised, to guide him to safety
  • Act 4 Scene 1
  • Edgar is waiting in the house thinking about his situation, he seems pleased as his situation could be much worse
  • However his calm demeanor is shattered when he sees Gloucester approaching, blinded
  • Gloucester tells him that he would give anything just to touch his son Edgar again, however Edgar does not choose to reveal himself
  • Gloucester asks Edgar (Tom) to take him to Dover, when Edgar agrees Gloucester adds that he wants to be taken to the highest cliff
  • Act 4 Scene 2
  • Goneril and Edmund finally arrive at Goneril's palace, however they are not met by Albany as Goneril expected
  • Oswald meets the two and tells them three things, first, Albany is upset with Regan and Goneril's actions, second that he is glad to hear the French army has landed, and third that he is sorry that Goneril is returning home
  • Goneril is very disappointed that her husband and her aren't allies anymore
  • She sends Edmund to Cornwall's palace to raise his troops to fight the French, she then explains her plan to take power back from her husband, finally she bids Edmund farewell with a kiss, perhaps hinting at future relations
  • Albany finally confronts Goneril about what occurred at Gloucester's house, the two then argue over each others actions
  • A messenger arrives and informs the two that Cornwall has recently died due to the wound he suffered earlier
  • Albany is pleased because of what Cornwall did to Gloucester, while Goneril has mixed feelings, she is pleased because now Regan is less powerful, however she is also worried that Regan will now pursue Edmund
  • Goneril leaves to respond to her sisters letters, meanwhile Albany learns that Edmund was the one who betrayed his father and left the castle so his father could be tortured
  • Act 4 Scene 3
  • The play shifts to Kent who, still in disguise, has made it to the French camp and given his letters to Cordelia
  • Kent has learned that the French king has left troops to deal with a matter back home
  • Kent learns from some of the troops about Cordelia's responses to the letters, which she was very sad about her father's treatment
  • Kent tells the men that Lear has made his way to Dover, however he is on the verge of madness and does not want to meet Cordelia out of shame
  • The group then learns that Albany and Cornwall's troops are on the move heading towards Dover to fight the French
  • Act 4 Scene 4
  • Cordelia arrives with her troops and sends them to find Lear
  • She consults with a doctor about his sanity and how to handle him when they find him, they decide he must rest and give him medicine
  • She then learns of the approaching armies and readies her troops
  • Act 4 Scene 5
  • The play then shifts back to Gloucester's castle where Oswald discusses with Regan about the armies movements
  • Oswald then brings a letter to Edmund from Goneril, however he does not show Regan despite her protests
  • Regan believes that it has to do with an affair between Edmund and Goneril
  • She then declares to Oswald that she intends to take Edmund for herself
  • Regan explains that since her husband has just died it would make more sense if she were to have a relationship with Edmund than Goneril who is still married
  • Regan than gives a letter to Oswald for Edmund and then departs after offering him a reward for murdering Gloucester
  • Act 4 Scene 6
  • Edgar takes Gloucester toward Dover, and on his way pretends to lead him toward a cliff as Gloucester desired
  • However the cliff that Edgar leads him to is not really a cliff, instead Edgar describes the scenery to make Gloucester believe he is standing on the edge of a cliff
  • Gloucester, thinking he can end his suffering, steps forward only to fall to the ground and faint
  • Edgar wakes up Gloucester following this, however instead of pretending to be the poor Tom, he is now pretending to be an ordinary gentleman, he still doesn't give away that he is Edgar
  • Edgar goes on to tell him that he witnessed him falling from the cliffs of Dover and survived, he adds that the "man" at the top of the cliff that was guiding him was not really a man but a devil
  • Gloucester now believes that he was saved by a divine power and that he is meant to still be alive
  • The two decide to continue on, and now come across Lear who is wandering about deliriously
  • He approaches the two and seems to babble and rant, eventually going on a tirade about sexuality
  • Cordelia's men arrive on stage and come across Lear, they then subdue him so they can return him back to Cordelia
  • Meanwhile, Oswald, who was with the troops, comes across Gloucester and Edgar
  • Oswald does not recognize Edgar, however he knows who Gloucester is and plans to murder him for the reward from Regan
  • Edgar has now taken a different disguise as a different peasant
  • He defends Gloucester and attacks and kills Oswald
  • Before Oswald can die, he hands his letters to Edgar to pass them on
  • Edgar decides to read the letters he has acquired, one is from Goneril to Edmund instructing him to murder Albany so that they can be together
  • Edgar plans to keep the note to show to Albany instead, he then buries Oswald before taking Gloucester away to safety
  • Act 4 Scene 7
  • Back at the French camp Cordelia speaks with Kent, who she now has recognized
  • Kent pleads that she does not give away his appearance
  • Cordelia then has Lear brought in for the first time
  • Lear explains that he is now aware that he is not in his right mind, he then goes on to apologize for his attitude and says that he would not be surprised if Cordelia wanted to kill him
  • Cordelia tells him that she doesn't want him dead, and that she forgives him
  • It is then learned that Cornwall has died and that Edmund has taken over his troops, the war is fast approaching
  • Act 5 Scene 1
  • The play goes back to the British camp where Regan and Edmund are alone
  • Regan questions Edmund about his relationship with Edmund, wondering whether he loves her and if the two have slept together, however Edmund denies both
  • Regan then goes on to tell of her jealousy for her sister, she also demands that Edmund not have relations with her from now on
  • The two are then interrupted by the arrival of Goneril, Albany and his troops
  • Albany tells the group that he has heard Lear has joined the French side along with some others
  • He adds that even though he does not despise Lear, he still plans to fight along side the rest of the group against the French
  • Goneril and Regan continue their argument over Edmund, and they both refuse to leave the other alone with him
  • Edgar arrives at the camp still disguised and approaches Albany showing him the letter from Goneril to Edmund
  • Edgar provides a plan for Albany to defend himself after the war from Edmund who plans to murder him
  • Edgar then leaves and Edmund arrives to meet Albany telling him the battle is about to begin
  • Edmund is then alone on stage, he tells the audience that he has sworn his love to both Goneril and Regan yet he does not know what to do
  • He adds that he will make his decision after the battle, hoping that Albany dies so he does not have to kill him
  • The scene closes with Edmund stating that he plans no mercy for Cordelia and Lear
  • Act 5 Scene 2
  • The battle finally begins and Edgar joins Lear's side
  • Shortly after it begins Edgar escapes the fighting to find Gloucester
  • He tells him that Lear's side has lost and both Cordelia and Lear have been captured
  • Gloucester resigns himself to death once more, yet again Edgar convinces him against it
  • Act 5 Scene 3
  • Edmund brings Lear and Cordelia in as prisoners and gives them to a guard with instructions, however the audience is unaware of what they orders are
  • Cordelia plans to confront her sisters if given a chance, yet Lear has given up
  • Albany then enters the stage with Goneril and Regan
  • He praises Edmund for his effort in the war and asks that he bring in Lear and Cordelia
  • Edmund lies to Albany and tells him that he has instead sent the two far away out of fear that they would inspire a mutiny
  • Albany is upset with Edmund and claims he had no right to make those orders
  • Regan interrupts Albany and declares that Edmund will be her husband
  • Goneril then interjects and demands that the marriage will not occur, but Regan, who suddenly falls ill, continues in her demands that Edmund will now be her husband
  • Albany jumps back in and arrests Edmund for treason, offering him a chance to defend himself in a duel
  • Albany then sounds a trumpet, as Edgar planned, to summon his champion to fight Albany
  • Regan is led away before the duel begins, and Edgar arrives in full armor to fight Edmund
  • Sure enough Edgar defeats Edmund, and only allows him to live at Albany's request
  • Goneril attempts to help out the wounded Edmund, but Albany confronts her over the letter and threatens her causing her to flee quickly
  • Edmund finally reveals himself to Albany and the rest of the people who witness the duel
  • He also explains that before the duel he revealed himself to Gloucester for the first time and that his father died after being told out of grief and joy
  • Suddenly he is interrupted by someone rushing in the room frantically announcing that Goneril has killed herself and that Regan has been fatally wounded before Goneril died
  • Kent arrives and asks what Edmund has done with Lear and Cordelia
  • Albany also demands that he give up their whereabouts
  • Edmund resolves to do some good before he is put to death and tells Albany that he had them sent away and Cordelia was to be hanged
  • A messenger is quickly sent to rescue her however he is too late
  • Lear enters carrying Cordelia's dead body and speaking incoherently
  • Kent tries to talk to him but Lear is obviously disturbed
  • It is quickly learned that Edmund is now dead as well
  • Lear makes one last attempt to save his daughter by loosing her blouse, and it appears as if she begins to breathe again, however Lear dies simultaneously
  • Albany restores Edgar and Kent with their power, yet Kent refuses
  • The play end as a funeral procession begins
