Themes

  • Making amends
  • At the beginning of the play King Lear divides his estate between two of his daughters leaving one of them, Cordelia, out of the inheritance
  • Cordelia loved her father very much, but was always blunt and honest with him, which upset him causing her to be left out
  • King Lear throughout the course of the novel realizes the errors of his ways, and he also learns that Cordelia's attitude towards him was only out of love
  • King Lear must reconcile with his daughter and manages to gain back her affection
  • Authority Vs. Chaos
  • In King Lear's old age he chooses to pass down his authority to two of his daughters
  • The daughters he leaves in power are reckless and terrible leaders
  • Authority is left in unworthy hands and the whole country seems to fall into chaos
  • The reader sees that strong authority in the wrong hands can lead to a great downfall and ultimately chaos
  • King Lear's relinquishing of his own authority leads to the further destruction of all authority in Great Britain
  • Lear must find a way to regain authority in his country and save it from chaos
  • Justice
  • The play is filled with terrible and tragic events that leaves one wondering if their is in fact any justice in what occurs
  • Many of the characters believe that the Gods are extremely unfair and unjust, as they leave those on earth to suffer at their whim
  • Others believe that the Gods are in fact just and that those who suffer these tragic acts are in fact deserving of their punishment
  • Throughout the entire play this debate over justice is seen, many seemingly horrible people suffer, as well as many innocent and undeserving people suffer as well
  • At the end all the suffering that ensues it is hard to determine whether these acts are just
