Basic Info

  • Title
  • King Lear
  • Author
  • William Shakespeare
  • Published
  • England, 1605. Published first by John Heminge and Henry Condell
  • Genre
  • Play, Tragedy
  • Setting
  • The play is set throughout England in the 8th century B.C.E.
  • Point Of View
  • Being that it is a play there is no particular point of view, however the play often focuses on King Lear, his daughters Goneril and Regan, and Edmund, a treacherous man who plots to destroy Lear and take over his land.
  • Conflict
  • The main conflict in the novel deals with Lear's willingness to trust his daughters simply because they state there love for him. In doing so he gives over his kingdom to his two worst daughters, and meanwhile casts away his most trustworthy daughter. His decision causes his Kingdom to fall apart and also causes King Lear to lose his sanity. In the end it is the trust he places in two of his daughters that leads to a tragic result.
