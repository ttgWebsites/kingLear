Book Summary

  • Considered one of Shakespeare's best plays, King Lear, is quite a tragedy. After dispersing his entire estate, in an effort to relinquish his power, among two of his three daughters, King Lear falls into an extreme state of madness. This madness seems as well as the decision to disinherit one of his three daughters seems to lead to a tragic downfall, not only for King Lear, but all involved.
